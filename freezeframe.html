FreezeFrame

FreezeFrame is a fast-paced, tactical puzzle-action game where time only moves when you do. This unique time-bending mechanic allows players to plan their next move while dodging attacks and lining up precise shots.

Challenges and Reflections

The development of FreezeFrame presented several challenges and learning opportunities. Below is a reflection on the difficulties faced during the creation of the game and the strategies employed to overcome them.

Challenges Faced

Some of the key challenges included:

  • Level generation and balancing difficulty consistently across randomly generated maps.
  • Enhancing the AI of enemies to make combat more engaging and less predictable.
  • Overhauling the weapon system to streamline interactions with different weapon types.
  • Managing version control more effectively to avoid merge conflicts and enhance team collaboration.

Solutions and Approaches

To address these challenges, we implemented the following solutions:

  • Improved level variety by adjusting the random generation algorithm to ensure diverse and balanced level designs.
  • Enhanced enemy AI by introducing more randomized behaviors and improved player tracking systems.
  • Simplified the weapon system by consolidating it into a single class, allowing for easier access to weapon-specific methods.

Reflection and Future Considerations

Looking back on the FreezeFrame development process, we identified several key takeaways:

  • Start planning version control workflows early to avoid common pitfalls like merge conflicts.
  • Ensure that core mechanics are thoroughly tested and refined before expanding the game's features.
  • Invest in more sophisticated AI design to keep gameplay challenging and engaging for players.
